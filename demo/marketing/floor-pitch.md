# Floor pitch (say this while handing over the QR)

## The 20 seconds

"I'm job hunting for real, so today I built the agency that runs it: Career Computa. Agents watch live company job boards, score fit against my actual resume, research the company with sources, tailor the resume, and write my LinkedIn outreach. The finished package lands in my Telegram and my only tap is the send. Scan this: sign up with your own resume or three quick fields and it will build a real package for you too. Everything it does is on a public ledger you can verify."

## The 3 most likely questions

**1. "Wait, it applies to jobs for you?"**

"It does everything up to the send: the tailored resume, the connection note, the DM draft, all apply-ready. I paste and send inside LinkedIn myself. That's deliberate: automated sending violates LinkedIn's terms, and the send is the one judgment call worth keeping human. And the tap is designed to disappear: approve a draft type enough times without editing and it graduates to ship without review. One click brings the review back."

**2. "What do I have to give it? Is my data safe?"**

"Your resume, or your LinkedIn export, or just three fields: current role, target role, target companies. Raw uploads are parsed and discarded, we keep only the derived profile, sensitive fields are masked by default, and there's a delete-my-data button, not a promise. Your package gets delivered to your own Telegram or a private link only you have."

**3. "How do I know the numbers are real?"**

"You don't have to trust me, that's the point of the ledger. Every completed task is a public row: the company, the role, the fit score, the cost, the duration. Hit verify on any row and it opens the live posting on the company's own job board, the full agent trace with the cost of every step, and the finished artifacts. Failures stay on the board too. The jobs come straight from Greenhouse, Lever, and Ashby public boards, so you can check any posting yourself right now."

## Bystander close

"Takes about two minutes to sign up and your package shows up while you watch. If it's good, tell me. If it's bad, hit not-for-me with a reason and you'll see the agency learn from it."
